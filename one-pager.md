# Evergreen Fund — One-Pager for RevHub

## The Problem

Social ventures fall through the cracks. Grants are too small, VC is too expensive. The "missing middle" ($50K–$1M) isn't a capital shortage — it's an *access* problem.

## The Solution

A revolving fund that reinvests its returns. Every dollar paid back goes right back out to fund the next founder. The fund never runs out.

## Why It Works

- **We target underestimated founders** — data shows they deliver 30% higher returns (McKinsey 2023)
- **Blended capital structure** — catalytic (first-loss) + patient + market-rate tranches attract different investors
- **Self-sufficient by Year 5** — returns cover operating costs, no constant fundraising needed

## Capital Structure

| Tranche | Source | Return | Role |
|---------|--------|--------|------|
| Catalytic | Foundations, DAFs | 0–2% | Absorbs early losses, signals credibility |
| Patient | Family offices, HNWIs | 4–8% | Core deployment capital |
| Market-Rate | Institutional LPs | 10–14% | Traditional VC-level returns |

## How Money Flows

1. **Deploy** → Capital goes to founders (recoverable grants, revenue-share, debt)
2. **Return** → Founders repay from revenue, exits, or follow-on funding
3. **Recycle** → Repayments go back into the pool, not to investors
4. **Reinvest** → New founders get funded. The cycle continues.

## Key Metrics

- **Fund Size:** $10M starting capital
- **Self-Sufficiency:** Year 5 (returns cover ops)
- **Recycling Rate:** 70%+ of capital redeployed
- **Breakeven:** The fund pays for itself by Year 5
