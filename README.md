### What is an Institutional LP?
An **Institutional Limited Partner (LP)** is a large, professional investor that commits capital to a private‑equity, venture‑capital, or other alternative‑asset fund. Unlike a “retail” LP (high‑net‑worth individuals), institutions manage pooled money on behalf of:

| Institution Type | Typical Assets Under Management |
|------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Pension Funds** (public‑sector, teacher unions) | $1 bn – $100 bn |
| **University Endowments** (Harvard, Yale, etc.) | $500 m – $50 bn |
| **Insurance Companies** (life, property & casualty) | $2 bn – $200 bn |
| **Family Offices** (multi‑family) | $100 m – $5 bn |
| **Sovereign Wealth Funds** (rare in our scope) | $10 bn – $1 trn |

They **sign a Limited Partnership Agreement** with the General Partner (GP) that runs the fund. The LP supplies the bulk of the capital, while the GP makes investment decisions and handles day‑to‑day operations.

---

### Why Institutional LPs Matter
- **Scale:** A single pension fund can write a **$5 m–$20 m check** in a fund, dwarfing the $100k‑$500k typical of a high‑net‑worth individual.
- **Stability:** Capital is often “locked‑in” for 10‑12 years, giving GPs a long runway for value creation.
- **Credibility:** Having a marquee institutional LP on the roster boosts a fund’s reputation and eases future fundraising.
- **Strategic Influence:** Institutions often bring governance expertise, industry contacts, and co‑investment opportunities.

### Key Considerations When Pitching to Institutional LPs
| Factor | What the LP Looks For | How to Deliver |
|--------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Track Record** | Consistent IRR > 15 % over 3‑5 years, clear exit stories | Highlight clean, audited performance tables |
| **Team Depth** | Experienced operators & operators with sector expertise | Show bios, tenure, and relevant past deals |
| **Risk Management** | Robust compliance, LP‑side reporting, ESG integration | Provide a concise risk‑framework diagram |
| **Alignment of Interests** | GP‑commitment (≥ 5 % of total capital) | State GP‑side “skin in the game” clearly |
| **Liquidity & Reporting** | Quarterly statements, transparent waterfall calculations | Offer a sample reporting deck |

### Typical Fund Structure (simplified)

```
+-------------------+          +-----------------------+
|   Institutional   |  LP     |    General Partner    |
|      LPs          +--------->|   (Fund Manager)      |
| (Pension, Endow.) |          |  (makes investments)  |
+-------------------+          +----------+------------+
                                            |
                                            v
                                   +-----------------+
                                   |  Portfolio Co.  |
                                   |  (Operating Cos)|
                                   +-----------------+
```

- **Capital Call** → LP sends cash → GP aggregates and deploys.
- **Management Fees** (2 % of committed capital) cover GP costs.
- **Carried Interest** (20 % of profits after hurdle) aligns incentives.

### Quick Checklist for a New Pitch Deck (Institutional LP‑focused)

1. **Executive Summary** – 1 slide, fund size, thesis, target returns.
2. **Team Slide** – Highlight GP’s institutional experience.
3. **Market Opportunity** – Data‑backed TAM/SAM.
4. **Investment Strategy** – Deal flow, stage, geography, check size.
5. **Track Record** – Past fund performance, IRR, multiples.
6. **Governance & Reporting** – LP‑side updates, ESG, risk controls.
7. **Terms Sheet** – Commitment size, fees, hurdle, GP‑commit.
8. **Closing Timeline** – Target close date, capital call schedule.
